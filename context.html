The framers of the Constitution designated Congress as the governmental power responsible for declaring war in Article 1, Section 8, Clause 11. "The Congress shall have Power To ... declare War..."
In one of the first wars following the establishment of the US Constitution, the War of 1812, Congress formally declared war with "An Act Declaring War between the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland and the Dependencies Thereof and the United States of America and Their Territories"
The last formal declaration of war by the United States was when the US joined WWII. The US began by declaring war on Japan following the Pearl Harbor attack on December 7 1941. Roosevelt, in his famous Infamy Speech, requested that Congress pass a declaration of war against Japan.
The final declaration of war by the US Congress would be on December 11 1941 with the US declaring war on both Germany and Italy a number of days following the declaration of war on Japan.
